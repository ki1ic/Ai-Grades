Introduction: The rise of artificial intelligence in education has transformed grading methodologies at both the high school and college levels. AI is reshaping the way assignments are assessed, making grading more efficient while also introducing concerns over academic honesty and fairness. According to Smith (2023), AI-driven assessment tools have enhanced grading speed but also led to debates about ethical implications.

Impact on High School

AI-powered tutoring programs and automated grading systems have become increasingly popular in high schools. These tools allow teachers to quickly assess studentsâ€™ work, but they also raise concerns about reliance on AI for learning. While AI can help students grasp difficult subjects, it may also reduce critical thinking skills if overused.

Impact on College

Colleges have seen a rise in AI-generated essays and AI-assisted research tools. Many students use AI for brainstorming and grammar correction, but some also use it to generate entire essays, leading to concerns about originality and authenticity. Professors are now using AI-powered plagiarism detection tools to maintain academic integrity.

Ethical Concerns

The ethical implications of AI in education are complex. On one hand, AI provides valuable learning support, but on the other, it raises serious concerns about cheating and academic dishonesty. Institutions are now implementing stricter guidelines on AI use in coursework to ensure that students maintain ethical standards while benefiting from AI advancements.
